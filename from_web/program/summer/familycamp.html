Holden Village on the Road

Find out what the Holden programming will look like!

Holden on the Road is an opportunity to sample life at Holden Village, a Lutheran retreat center located in the rugged North Cascade Mountains of Washington state. Every year thousands of people from around the world visit this remote, year-round community on the edge of the Glacier Peak Wilderness. Originally a mining town, Holden Village is being impacted by a massive, federally-mandated cleanup of pollution that remains from mining days. Unable to welcome guests because of heavy construction this summer, Holden is partnering with venues across the country to provide experiences similar to those in the Village. Partnering sites vary from spectacular natural settings to dynamic urban neighborhoods. In all cases, programming will be diverse and stimulating, emphasizing multigenerational and family involvement.

As at Holden, the program will include options for outdoor recreation, arts and crafts, Bible study, educational sessions, and communal daily worship. Participants can also learn about Holden, its mission and values, and its emphasis on environmental stewardship during the disruption of mine remediation. We look forward to welcoming longtime and new Holden community members to Holden on the Road.

Adult Bible Study
Pilgrimage: Journey's of Faith in Scripture and Daily Life
From Abraham and Sarah to Jesus and the Apostle Paul, the Bible is filled with exciting and often dangerous journey's of faith. Explore these journeys as "pilgrimage" and discover how God continues to travel with us in our lives today.

Bible Study Leader: Rev. Dr. Dave Ellingson, is Professor of Children, Youth & Family Studies at Trinity Lutheran College, near Everett, WA. He teaches courses in spirituality, social justice, environmental theology, and human development. For many years Dave has coordinated both the October Let Justice Roll event and the May Youth Weekends at Holden Village. On a recent sabbatical he paddled the entire Mississippi River in his kayak and blogged as "paddle pilgrim".
