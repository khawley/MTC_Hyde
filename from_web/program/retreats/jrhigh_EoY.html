Please note that each individual family must register their youth, NOT the youth leader! ALL adults MUST sign up too!

The EOY [End of the Year] Retreat is a weekend program for Confirmation aged youth and is designed to let them explore their faith. Facilitated by our dynamic speakers, special music, and thought provoking breakout sessions, youth participants will read 1 Corinthians 13 and explore what it means to LIVE -2- LOVE.

Theme:


"Love is patient, love is kind. It does not envy, it does not boast, it is not proud. 5 It does not dishonor others, it is not self-seeking, it is not easily angered, it keeps no record of wrongs. 6 Love does not delight in evil but rejoices with the truth. 7 It always protects, always trusts, always hopes, always perseveres. [Love] always protects, always trusts, always hopes, always perseveres.” 1 Corinthians 13:7


EoY Speaker: Ralph Supper

Ralph Supper (pronounced Super) has played many different roles in his life, but has always stayed true to his mission: to know God and make God known. He has an overwhelming optimism and faith. Ralph has been a pastor for several years and is currently speaking at various events and working on a graphic-driven book while working full time supporting people with autism. Ralph’s positivity, humor, and innovative approach brings a newness to Christ’s message and excites the community to get involved in ministry. His self-proclaimed life verse is from Nehemiah, “The joy of the Lord is my strength”.

Music: Katey Wade

Katey Wade began leading music in high school for her church and Lutheran Retreats, Camps and Conferences. These experiences led her to California Lutheran University where she received 2 degrees in Religious Studies and Music, with a focus in Church Music and Vocal Performance. She was involved in the CLU Choir and Vocal Quartets for all four years and led the student congregation in music on a weekly basis. After graduating CLU, she became an Outdoor School Instructor and Music Leader for LRCC's year-round staff. She also helped lead the music for the annual Youth Workers Extravaganza in Kansas City. She feels closest to God when she is surrounded my music, nature and giggling children of all ages. She currently works as a Social Service Worker for the Homeless Advocacy Project in San Francisco after she did a year of volunteer service through the Lutheran Volunteer Corps.


Housing & Adult Leaders: All participants will be housed in Evergreen/Redwood dorms and Madrone cabins. These buildings have bunk beds and shared bathroom facilities. Adult leaders will stay with youth from their church and youth from other churches as well. Each church group needs a male and female adult leader if they bring male & female youth. Must have at least 1 adult leader per 7 youth.
